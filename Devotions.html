The Believer and Prayer.

Something to learn here , you'll notice everything the will of God is mentioned, prayer or praying is in view

Matthew 26:39 And he went a little farther, and fell on his face, and PRAYED, saying, O my Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me: nevertheless not as I will, but as thou wilt.

Thus, we see in prayers or praying we're partnering with God's will for the earth.

Another example

1 Timothy 2:4 Who will have all men to be saved, and to come unto the knowledge of the truth.

Notice God our savior will is that all men be saved? Yet in verse 1 of same chapter, prayer is in view

1 Timothy 2:1 I exhort therefore, that, first of all, supplications, prayers, intercessions, and giving of thanks, be made for all men;

In verse 3 he said this is "Good" Kalos in the Greek, used for what is proper and fitting. Used in Mathew 3:10. This is proper, acceptable, in God's face, sight and presence.

Another case

Matthew 6:9 After this manner therefore pray ye: Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy name.

After this manner, look at verse 2

Matthew 6:10 Thy kingdom come. Thy will be done in earth, as it is in heaven.

They kingdom come, is God's will on earth and the manner is prayers.

Thus anytime we pray, God's will is done on the earth, God's kingdom reigns on the earth. So, prayer is the manner we partner.

Notice the opposite of this kingdom

Luke 22:53 When I was daily with you in the temple, ye stretched forth no hands against me: but this is your hour, and the power of darkness

Notice, stretching forth their hands against him is called "the power or kingdom" of darkness. In Acts we see something similar

Acts 12:1 Now about that time Herod the king stretched forth his hands to vex certain of the church.
Acts 12:2 And he killed James the brother of John with the sword.

He didn't stop there

Acts 12:3 And because he saw it pleased the Jews, he proceeded further to take Peter also. (Then were the days of unleavened bread.)

But we see that God's kingdom rule isn't automatic, thus prayers was made in the case of Peter

Acts 12:5 Peter therefore was kept in prison: but prayer was made without ceasing of the church unto God for him.

So, you see it wasn't God's unique need or desire for James to die.
